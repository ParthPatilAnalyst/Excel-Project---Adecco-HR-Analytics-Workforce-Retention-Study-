
# Adecco HR Analytics: Workforce Retention Study

## Project Overview

This project focuses on workforce analytics to understand **employee attrition patterns** and **job satisfaction drivers** within **Adecco India**.
Employee turnover creates direct costs through hiring, training, and lost productivity. This analysis uses employee demographic data and feedback metrics to identify the main reasons behind attrition and to support data-driven retention strategies.

The project is designed to help **HR leaders and management teams** make informed decisions to improve employee engagement and long-term retention.

---

## Business Problem Statement

Employee attrition negatively affects:

* Organizational stability
* Team performance
* Recruitment and training costs
* Employee morale

Adecco India required a structured, analytical approach to move beyond assumptions and clearly understand:

* Why employees leave
* Which factors influence job satisfaction
* Which employee groups are at higher risk of attrition

---

## STAR Method Breakdown

### Situation

High employee turnover was impacting productivity and increasing operational costs. Although exit interviews existed, there was no consolidated, data-backed view of attrition drivers. HR teams needed clear insights into how compensation, workload, distance from home, and job roles influenced employee retention.

---

### Task

The key objectives of this project were:

* Calculate and track overall employee attrition
* Identify department-wise attrition trends
* Analyze the impact of job satisfaction, income, overtime, and travel on attrition
* Detect high-performing employees who were at risk of leaving
* Present findings in an interactive and easy-to-understand format for HR teams

---

### Action

A systematic HR analytics approach was followed to achieve the objectives.

#### KPI Definition and Measurement

* Calculated the **overall attrition rate of 16.1%**
* Segmented attrition by department, such as:

  * Human Resources
  * Research and Development
* Compared attrition patterns across different job roles

#### Correlation and Driver Analysis

* Analyzed the relationship between attrition and key variables:

  * Distance from Home
  * Monthly Income
  * Overtime
* Identified patterns indicating which factors had the strongest influence on employee turnover

#### Dashboard Development

* Designed an **interactive HR dashboard** to:

  * Filter employee satisfaction scores by job role
  * Compare roles such as Sales Executive and Healthcare Representative
  * Support quick, data-driven HR decisions

#### Tenure and Performance Mapping

* Analyzed **average tenure of 7.01 years**
* Mapped tenure against performance ratings
* Identified high-performing employees with low satisfaction scores who were at risk of leaving

---

### Result

The analysis produced clear and actionable outcomes:

* **Overtime** and **Monthly Income** were identified as the two most critical factors contributing to employee attrition
* Employees who traveled frequently showed **lower job satisfaction scores**
* These insights provided the foundation for:

  * Revising overtime policies
  * Reviewing compensation structures
  * Improving corporate travel policies
* HR teams gained the ability to proactively identify and engage at-risk employees before resignation
